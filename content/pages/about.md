I'm a software engineer, but really I'm just someone who can't leave a good puzzle alone. Hand me a
problem — ideally a slightly annoying one — and I'll happily disappear into it until it gives up.
The best part of the job is that "solved" usually means someone's day just got a little easier.

## Tinkerer by default

I'm a DIY tinkerer at heart. If something can be taken apart, improved, or over-engineered _just a
little_, odds are I've already started. I build with Lego too — I'll follow the instructions more
often than I'd like to admit, with the occasional MOC when I'm feeling brave.

Fair warning: scope creep and I go way back. "Quick weekend project" is a phrase I use purely for
its comedic value.

## Always learning something

New things are fun — and it genuinely doesn't matter how trivial. I'll cheerfully vanish down a
rabbit hole to learn something I may never use again, mostly because _not_ knowing it was starting
to bother me.

## Away from the keyboard

When I'm not building something, I'm usually travelling, or being kept happily busy by my wife,
kids, and dog — not necessarily in that order.

And honestly, one of my favourite things to do is just watch. Movies and TV to unwind, but mostly
hours of YouTube — people building things and picking up skills that I fully intend to use someday,
plus the lighter stuff that helps me switch off. Call it research; it's usually just fun.

Curious about something I didn't mention? Just ask.
